Fixed Orthodontics

TYPE A: Clinical fixed orthodontics program - 6 months long term / continuous 12 days

This program is for beginners to learn over 6 months on a long-term basis, with 2 days per month for a total of 12 modules. It covers cases at different stages, all exercises on typhodonts and models, and includes post-program mentorship and online support during and after the program. Participants can also simultaneously take up cases in their clinics.

120 hours program.

Theory: 60 hours in PowerPoint.

Hands-on: 60 hours on typhodonts and patients.

For dates, call us and book your convenient dates to come and learn in Kerala, INDIA.

About the Program

This is a 6 months program of 2 days per month in 12 modules .This program will be with detailed power point presentation of more than 3500 slides covering various topics in fixed orthodontics in straight wire,functional,orthopaedic,preventive and interceptive orthodontics,mini ortho implants,selfligating,lingual and other latest fixed orthodontic techiniques.More than 50 casts,100 case discussions and treatment planning ,wire bending soldering ,welding, appliances manufacturing exercises are included in this program. The elaborate case discussions and hands for any general dentist as a best package to learn fixed orthodontic .You can also start your own cases in your clinic simultaneously with constant reporting ,a complete on line support will be extended in treatment planning and cases discussions thereafter.

Includes lecture ,hands on training on 1 acrylic and 3 wax typhodonts .100 completed modules and batches. Over 2000 dentists trainedyou get text books CD,study material, running notes, slides synopsis, videos,you get typodonts exercises patient cases,pliers and orthodontic material for hands on to do.free post program mentorship in treatment planning, trouble shooting, online support & updates for unlimited period through ADSC Google group.

Six Modules In Six Months

  • Introduction - history of orthodontics, types of appliances - removable & fixed.
  • Clinical examination - extra oral, intra oral, functional.
  • Etiology of malocclusion and classifications of malocclusion - dental, skeletal.

    • Hands-on: Bonding, banding exercises on typhodont & patient cases.

  • Growth and development – maxilla & mandible, adolescent growth spurt detection.
  • Model analysis, X-rays, OPG, cephalometry, discussion on study models.
  • Digital dental photography and data processing techniques.

    • Hands-on: Wire bending exercises, fabrication of removable appliances, welding and soldering. Bonding, banding exercises.

  • Treatment planning-space analysis ,extraction & non extraction philosophy- extraction choice ,which teeth and why non extraction methods- distalization,uprighting,derotion,expansion,stripping,serial extraction etc.
  • Biomechanics -of tooth movement.
  • Armamentarium required – consumable, nonconsumables.

    • Hands-on: Model analysis ,bonding, banding and wiring.

  • Wires – types,properties,metlurgy.
  • Anchorage -controle,types.
  • Leveling and aligning in fixed orthodontics.
  • Functional appliances and clinical application.

    • Hands-on: Patient cases follow-up and exercises wire changing & wire selection

  • Straight wire technique-(MBT)-introduction ,phylosophy,history,mechanics,anchorage controle,materials,stages-I,II,bonding ,banding, wiring,welding,soldering,wire sequence and selection.
  • Overbite controle,overjet reduction, space closure, finishing ,detailing,debondig,patient care.
  • Case discussion of all types cl–l,ll,lll,case discussion of all types on models ,photographes,casts.

    • Hands-on: Wire changing ,patient case discussions ,twin block, bite planes fabrication and delivery in patient.

  • Concept of tip edge,Beggs,selfligating,lingual and clear orthodontics
  • Surgical orthodontics, retention and relapse.
  • Interceptive, preventive and adult orthodontics.
  • pre prosthetic orthodontics,perio ortho considerations 21.LINGUAL ORTHODONTICS –Introduction and history of Lingual Orthodontics Case selection advantage and disadvantage of lingual orthodontics Bracket Design, Bracket selection,Various lingual techniques:-Hiro,TARG,CLARS,STB,Incongita lingual orthodontics indirect bonding 2D and 3D. Biomechanics, Stages of treatment,Patient management, wire bending, wire sequence, retention.
  • MINI IMPLANTS -How ,when ,where Hands on -2D lingual direct and indirect bonding- Hiro,STB, lingual, wire bending and changing. demonstration of push coils, pull coils, tie back, rocking chair curves ,mini implants placement, wire changing ,patient case discussions ,deboning and retention appliance delivery
